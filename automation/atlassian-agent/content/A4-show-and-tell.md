# 🎤 Show & Tell — Demo Accountability

Everyone demos their own work — every sprint, every role, including small tasks. This is how contributions get *seen* (credit), how quality gets *verified* (the demo can't lie), and how knowledge spreads without meetings. It's not a performance review; it's the team's highlight reel. Think of it like a cooking class 👨‍🍳 where everyone plates their own dish — the plating takes two minutes, and everyone leaves knowing five new recipes.

## 1. The format (inside Sprint Review — no new meeting)

- **2–3 minutes per person**, hard-timed. Demo the *working thing*, not slides.
- The owner of the item demos it — not the lead, not a proxy. Pairs demo together.
- Non-code roles demo too: a BA walks one spec that survived Three Amigos unchanged; a DBA shows the restore-test result; an SRE shows the dashboard that caught something; a DevOps engineer runs the pipeline live.
- Small tasks count: "I standardised .gitignore on two repos — here's the diff and the secret-scan now passing" is a perfect demo.
- The PO accepts items at the demo, on the spot, against the acceptance criteria.

## 2. Demo prep checklist (attach to your item before review day)

- [ ] One sentence: what problem this solved and for whom
- [ ] The S.C.A.L.E.D. short block filled on the item (Scale, Compliance, Action, Leverage, Effect, Diffusion)
- [ ] Working demo path tested 30 minutes before (environment up, data ready)
- [ ] Evidence already linked: PR, pipeline run, test results
- [ ] The "Effect" number ready (time saved, errors removed, coverage added — one metric)
- [ ] 30-second fallback if the live demo breaks: screenshot or recording

> The checklist takes ~10 minutes if your item followed the process — every artifact already exists on the item. If prep feels hard, that's the system showing you a gap: evidence was skipped earlier. Fix the habit, not the demo.

## 3. Rules that keep it humane

- Time is enforced kindly but firmly — 3 minutes, then next. Depth goes to follow-up items, not overruns.
- Questions allowed: 1 per demo in the room; the rest go as comments on the item.
- Failed demos are fine and normal — show what you learned, raise the fix as an item. The only failure is having nothing on the item.
- Nobody is compared to anybody. The demo log (below) is a record of contribution, never a leaderboard.

## 4. Demo log

Keep a simple running list per sprint at the bottom of this page (or a `Demos` database if you prefer): Date · Who · Item · One-line outcome · Recording link. Three months in, this log is every person's self-written evidence of impact — useful for growth conversations they control.

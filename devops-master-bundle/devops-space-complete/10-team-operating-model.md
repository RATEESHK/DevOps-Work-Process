This page defines how the team works day-to-day: meeting discipline, focus time, communication rules, and the no-overtime principle. The aim is maximum productive time per person, fast releases, and work people actually enjoy.

## 1. Core working principles

- **Async first.** Default to a written update (work item comment, Confluence, channel post) before booking a meeting.
- **No lengthy calls.** Every meeting has an agenda, a timebox, and only the people who are needed. No agenda = no meeting.
- **Come prepared.** Attendees bring their questions written down. Concerns raised in a meeting that can't be resolved in the room become backlog items — the meeting does not extend.
- **Focus blocks.** Each person protects at least one 2–3 hour uninterrupted block daily. No meetings are booked into team focus hours (suggested: 09:30–12:00 local).
- **No overtime** unless urgent and necessary (Sev-1 incident, regulatory deadline). If overtime happens, the cause is logged and reviewed at the retro so it doesn't repeat.
- **Freedom of choice.** People pick up work they're interested in where possible; stretch tasks are encouraged. Nothing is forced — growth comes from interest, and even small contributions are visible and credited (every item has an owner whose name is on the delivered work).
- **Evidence as you go.** Documentation and proof are attached while doing the work, not after — this is what keeps everyone in sync without status calls and lets releases happen without delays.

## 2. Communication channels — which to use when

| Need | Channel | Response expectation |
|---|---|---|
| Status of work | Work item comment | Same day |
| Standards / how-to | Confluence (this space) | Self-serve |
| Quick question | Team channel (not DM) | Within a few hours |
| Decision needed | Work item + Decision Log | Per escalation path |
| Blocker | Flag the item + tag owner | Same day |
| Urgent / Sev-1 | On-call channel + phone | Immediate |

Personal chats are never the system of record. A decision made in chat is copied to the Decision Log the same day.

## 3. Meeting rules

1. Agenda published in the invite at least a few hours before; otherwise decline is acceptable.
2. Timeboxes: standup 15 min, refinement 60 min, planning 90 min, review 60 min, retro 60 min, Three Amigos 30 min per story batch, ad-hoc 25 min default.
3. Required attendees only; everyone else gets the notes.
4. Outputs written into the work item or Confluence before the meeting ends; new concerns become backlog items immediately.
5. Recurring meetings are reviewed quarterly — any meeting that consistently produces no decisions or items is cancelled.

## 4. Collaboration without calls (the 24/7-style flow)

The goal of "continuous output without compromising quality" is achieved by handoffs and automation, not long hours:

- Work items carry full context (template + S.C.A.L.E.D.), so anyone in any timezone can pick up where another left off.
- CI/CD gates do the verification a human would otherwise be woken up for.
- The board is the single status view — nobody needs a call to know where things stand.
- Daily interaction needed per person: standup (15 min) + responding to tags (≤30 min) + any booked ceremony. Everything else is focus time.

## 5. Satisfaction and recognition

- Sprint review explicitly names contributors per delivered item, including small tasks.
- Retro includes a "what did you enjoy / what do you want more of" question to route future work by interest.
- Each person maintains a lightweight growth goal (see Roles, Goals & KPIs) reviewed monthly with their lead — aimed at becoming strong across the stack over time, at their own pace.
